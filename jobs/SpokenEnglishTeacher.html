Spoken English Teacher Skills:


Teaching is considered a popular, rewarding and fulfilling profession as teachers help develop skills and knowledge in students. The job of an English teacher can be demanding as it requires a specific set of skills to succeed in this role. Understanding the skills and characteristics of a successful English teacher can help you prepare for this career. In this article, we discuss the English teacher skills, share some ways to develop them and explain how to highlight these skills in your job application.


1. Communication skills

English teachers require having an excellent understanding of both verbal and non-verbal communication. For a significant part of their day, English teachers have to deliver lessons, interact with students and supervise them. Teachers also communicate with parents, administration and colleagues, both in writing and verbally. Strong communication skills can also make classes more engaging and interactive for students.


2. Patience

It can be challenging to teach a new language to non-native speakers. Instilling the habit of using the language in everyday life can require a lot of patience from trainers and teachers. Furthermore, students can be from various cultural backgrounds and have different intellectual abilities. This requires creating an engaging environment to provide an equal and fair learning experience. Teachers may also repeat sessions or key concepts to ensure that students understand key concepts. All of this requires tremendous patience and discipline from teachers.


3. Empathy

Empathy is being aware of and understanding how others feel. As an English teacher, it is an important skill to have. Being empathic towards students helps teachers understand their problems from a new perspective. Empathy can help the teacher prevent anger, be patient to understand the reason and put effort to make things easier for the student. Empathy is also essential for teachers to manage students that come from different socio-economic and cultural backgrounds.


4. Organisational skills

The role of an English teacher includes making lesson plans, conducting classes, supervising activities and performing assessments. They may also perform several other jobs including, maintaining student records and generating report cards. With so many duties to manage, it is crucial for teachers to organise their work and maintain a healthy work-life balance. Organisational skills also help a teacher keep track of student records, lessons, learning progress and student assessment reports of several students.


5. Computer skills

Teachers use computers to track student progress, create worksheets, make examination papers and do other administrative tasks. They may also use devices in the classroom to teach English to students through videos, and they may also require to use computers and projectors if they teach in smart classrooms. They often research online to stay up-to-date on teaching methodologies or other teaching-related tasks. This makes basic computer skills essential for teachers to perform these tasks effectively.


6. Lesson planning

English teachers create lesson plans for their students by combining various teaching methodologies that allow maximum transfer and retention of knowledge and prepare students for examinations. This may require research, planning and organising topics in a manner that is the most effective for different student groups. A well-designed lesson plan contains practical projects, important basic literature and classroom activities that can engage students and help them learn the language.


7. Intercultural sensitivity

English teachers interact with students from different backgrounds. They understand and value all cultures to create an inclusive classroom environment. They try to ensure that no course material is insensitive to cultural differences. Teachers knowing multiple languages can help students learn and understand English better.


8. Teamwork

Large schools and institutions may employ multiple English teachers. There can also be other departments working within the institute. Apart from taking classes, it is vital for teachers to communicate with their colleagues to discuss lesson plans, student performance, administrative tasks and school affairs. Teachers who have good teamwork skills can perform their duties better and help students learn English better.


9. Adaptability

Adaptability refers to how well someone responds and adjusts to change. It is important for teachers to be adaptable to unanticipated changes, like classroom interruptions or modifications in the academic curriculum. Being adjustable requires flexibility, the ability to improve performance according to the feedback and commitment to updating the most effective learning approach.


10. Critical thinking

Teachers having strong critical thinking skills are more likely to help their students develop skills, values and knowledge. As teachers can make a significant impact on the impressionable minds of young students, having critical thinking skills allows teachers to encourage their students to learn things beyond the academic curriculum. Teachers with strong critical thinking skills can help students introspect, understand complex ideas and form objective opinions.


11. Time management

The responsibilities and duties of a teaching role can extend outside the classroom. Teachers may work extra hours to make lesson plans, assess papers, prepare teaching-learning materials and supervise extra-curricular activities. To keep a balance between work and your personal life, you may require to develop effective time management skills. Effective time management skills can help you accomplish your tasks efficiently and prioritise them.
